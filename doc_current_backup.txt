The Headline
GSO is operating at 74% of demand capacity. The team completes ~612 DSRs/month against ~824 inbound, creating a structural deficit of ~212 DSRs/month. This has caused the active pipeline to balloon from 148 in January to 997 today — a 6.7x increase in one quarter. Quality remains excellent (4.73/5 CSAT), but without intervention, the pipeline will exceed 2,200 by end of Q2 and lead times will begin degrading.

What We Did Well
Before diving into bottlenecks and gaps, it is worth calling out what is working — because Q1 had real, measurable wins that validate the direction GSO is heading.

The Project Management Model Is Proving Out
The biggest story of Q1 is not the pipeline growth — it is the emergence of a new operating model. The top-performing reps adopted a project management approach: instead of personally executing every training, installation, and site survey, they dispatched the majority of hands-on work to 3P partners and focused their time on seller relationship management, scheduling, and driving DSRs to completion.

The data is unambiguous. Reps who dispatched more partner DSAs per DSR completed more DSRs and did it faster:

High partner utilization (1.28+ DSAs per DSR): 15 reps averaging 19.1 completions/month
Low partner utilization (below 1.28 DSAs per DSR): 14 reps averaging 16.1 completions/month

That is a 19% throughput advantage for reps who leaned into the partner model. They are not working harder — they are working differently.

Austin Crittenden: The Model in Action
Austin had a Q1 for the record books and is the clearest proof point for the project management model:

76 DSRs completed (25.3/month) — 49% above the team average of 17/month
19-day median lead time — 42% faster than the GSO Q1 median of 32 days
68% of his DSRs closed within 30 days
$107M in GPV onboarded across his Q1 portfolio
109 partner DSAs dispatched in Q1 (1.43 per DSR) — he gave nearly every installation, training, and site survey to a partner

His work type mix was perfectly balanced: 20 Premium, 20 Optimization Calls, 20 Plus, 7 Onsite, 4 Payroll. He did not cherry-pick easy work — he ran the full complexity spectrum and still posted the fastest lead times on the team.

His partner dispatch breakdown: 43 to Absolute POS, 35 to F3 Technologies, 20 to Solio Marlow, 11 to Sora Partners. He used all four major partners and kept them fully loaded.

March was his breakout month: 42 completions against 50 created — nearly 1:1 throughput-to-inbound while managing a 35-DSR active pipeline. That is elite-level pipeline management.

4. Quality Metrics
📊 Supporting data: "CSAT & Quality" tab in Throughput spreadsheet

Despite the capacity pressure, quality is holding:

CSAT: 4.73/5.0 — 94% rate 4+ stars, only 4% detractors (7 of 187)
BPO: 1,529 activities/month, 91% completion rate
Late cancellations: 11/month (~132/year). A late cancellation is a partner-dispatched appointment (training, installation, or site visit) that is cancelled within 24 hours of the scheduled time. Absolute POS and Sora Partners account for ~50% of these, but the root cause is typically on the GSO/seller side — sellers cancel or go dark because expectations were not set properly during the onboarding handoff. This is a GSO process issue, not a partner performance issue.

Productivity impact: Of the 132 late cancellations over the past 12 months, 88 (67%) were cancelled after the scheduled time — meaning the partner tech was already deployed or waiting and the seller was a no-show. On average, these cancellations occurred 15 hours past the appointment time. Only 43 (33%) were cancelled before the scheduled time with any notice at all. At an average cost of ~$720 per partner activity, an estimated 85% of late-cancelled appointments cannot be rescheduled to another seller on the same day. That puts the annual wasted partner spend at ~$81,000 and ~560 lost tech-hours per year (equivalent to ~0.3 FTE of partner capacity sitting idle). 54 of the 132 were onsite appointments involving travel and physical prep — these carry the highest per-cancel cost.

Tighter seller-readiness validation before scheduling partner appointments would reduce these significantly. A simple pre-appointment confirmation 48 hours out — verifying the seller has hardware, network access, and a confirmed point of contact — could eliminate the majority of no-shows

The team is protecting quality by letting the pipeline grow rather than rushing work. Right trade-off for now, but not sustainable past Q2.

Methodology & Data Notes

What's included in all metrics above:
GSO channel Deal Support Requests (DSRs) with Record Type = "GSO Global Seller Onboarding"
All work types: Onsite (25%), Optimization Call (23%), Premium (19%), Plus (17%), Payroll (4%), Tasks Only (4%), In-Person Refresh (1%), Appointments Migration (<1%), 3P Vendor Install (<1%)
"Created" = DSR created date falls in that month, regardless of current status
"Completed" = DSR reached a terminal status (Implementation Complete, Completed, or Transitioned) with completion date in that month — these DSRs may have been created in any prior period (64% were created same quarter, 32% prior quarter)
"Cancelled" = DSR reached Rejected, Lost - No Seller Contact, Lost - Churn, or Lost
"Net" = Created minus Completed minus Cancelled (represents pipeline growth/shrinkage)
"Pipeline" = Active + On Hold DSRs assigned to current roster reps only (excludes departed reps' orphaned DSRs)

What's excluded from all metrics above:
BPO channel (31 reps under Jennifer Walker) — 3,197 total records, 903 created in Q1. These are tracked separately in the BPO Activities tab.

Draft DSRs — 1,357 records. Drafts are incomplete submissions that haven't entered the workflow.
Combined: 6,959 records excluded from the 40,781 total in the system.
